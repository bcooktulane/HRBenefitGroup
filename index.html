What is the Affordable Care Act (Obamacare)?

The Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act was passed by Congress in 2010 and is will take effect in 2014 with early enrollment available in October of 2013. The law provides individuals and businesses with incentives and options to buy health insurance to reduce the number of people without coverage. Major components of the act include: tax credits to make insurance more affordable, guaranteed access to coverage, and government-run health insurance exchanges.

What is the SHOP Marketplace?

The Small Business Health Options Program (SHOP) will simplify the process of buying health insurance for your small business. It gives you more coverage choices and better control over costs. The experts at HR Benefit Group are paid by the government to help enroll people like you in the program. There is no additional cost when using a broker like HRBG and the government recommends having an advisor to to walk you through the choices for your small business.

Learn more about the SHOP Marketplace

What is considered a small business?

If you have fewer than 50 full time employees you're considered a small business under the new health care laws. As a small business may get insurance for your employees through the SHOP Marketplace. If you have fewer than 25 full time employees than you might qualify for a tax credit (we can help you apply for that!). If you're self employed or have no employees than you can buy insurance through the individual health insurance marketplace (we can also help you!).

How can Obamacare help me as an individual?

There are numerous ways to take advantage of the new Health Insurance Marketplace to provide better coverage and lower costs for you and your family. There is no cost to the consumer if you use a broker like HRBG, so let us do the legwork comparing plans and saving you money on health insurance.

Enroll as early as October 1, 2013.

The Affordable Care Act ensures that all health plans offered to the consumer include a comprehensive package of items and services known as essential health benefits. These types of plans are accessible both inside and outside of the Health Insurance Marketplace and are open to enrollment on 9/1/2013. More people than ever will qualify to save money on private insurance coverage.
